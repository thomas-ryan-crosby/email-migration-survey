📧 Help Us Help You: Email Setup Survey

We just moved everyone's email over to Google Workspace. To make sure you can keep reading and sending email on all your devices, we'd like to know how you check email today. This will take about 2 minutes.

First — what is an "email app"?

An email app (sometimes called an "email client" or "email management system") is simply the program you open to read and send your email. Think of it like a mailbox app: the mail itself lives at the post office (the email server), and the app on your device is how you go look at it.

You might use one app on your computer, a different one on your phone, and sometimes a website in a browser. All of them can show the same email account — they're just different windows into it.

🏤 What actually changed: the post office, not your app

The post office (the email server) is what moved — from Rackspace to Google Workspace. Your email address is the same. In most cases you do not need to change which app you use — you just need to point it at the new server.

A couple of notes:

• Many people find the Gmail app is a really nice fit with Google Workspace email, since they're made by the same company. If you've been curious about switching, now's a good time.

• If you were using Outlook only because the old Rackspace server didn't work with the Gmail app, that limitation is gone — Outlook is no longer required.

⭐ Our recommendation

No strong preference? Use Gmail.

If you don't have a favorite email app, we recommend Gmail at gmail.com on your computer and the Gmail app on your phone. Since both ends are run by Google, you'll get the smoothest experience — nothing to install on a computer, and the mobile app is built for this exact account type.

If you'd rather use the system you're already familiar with (Outlook, Apple Mail, etc.), no problem — just pick it in the common options below, and we'll point you to the exact guide for your situation.

Why we're asking: Each app needs slightly different steps to connect to the new Google Workspace email. If we know which ones you use, we can give you the exact step-by-step guide for your situation.
