Setup Guide

Adding your Crosby email to the new Outlook for Windows

Step-by-step walkthrough for connecting your @crosbydevelopment.com Workspace email to the new Outlook desktop app.

⏱ ~5 min 📝 Need: email + password 🔐 Have 2FA ready

Before you start

  • Open the new Outlook app — the icon is light blue and white. If you're using classic Outlook (darker blue square with a white envelope/O), use the classic Outlook guide instead.
  • If you've never had Crosby email on this PC, skip Phase 1 and go straight to Phase 2.
  • If your old crosbydevelopment.com account was already in Outlook (it was connecting through Rackspace), do Phase 1 first to remove it cleanly.

Phase 1 — Remove the old account (skip if you've never set up Crosby email here)

1

Open Settings

In the top-right corner of Outlook, click the ⚙ gear icon to open Settings.

2

Go to Accounts → Email accounts

On the left side of the Settings window, click Accounts, then Email accounts.

📧 Email accounts ›
🔔 Notifications ›
🗂 Storage ›
3

Find the old account

You'll see your existing accounts listed. Find the one labeled yourname@crosbydevelopment.com with "IMAP" or "POP" underneath it — that's the old Rackspace one.

Click Manage next to it.

4

Remove the account

On the Manage screen, click Remove at the bottom. Confirm when asked.

Don't worry about losing email — the messages live on the server. Removing the account here just disconnects this computer from the old server; we'll reconnect through the new Google server in Phase 2.

Phase 2 — Add your Workspace account

5

Open the Add account screen

Still in Settings → Accounts → Email accounts. Click + Add account at the top.

6

Enter your Crosby email

Type your full address — yourname@crosbydevelopment.com — and click Continue.

Suggested account
yourname@crosbydevelopment.com
Continue
7

Sign in to Google

A Google sign-in window opens (the page says accounts.google.com at the top). It already has your email filled in — click Next.

Seeing "Couldn't find your Google Account"? Double-check the spelling, then try again. If it still fails, your Workspace account may not be active yet — call us (numbers below).
8

Enter your password

Type the password for your Workspace account and click Next. If 2-factor authentication (2FA) is on, complete the verification step (text code, phone tap, or authenticator app).

9

Allow Outlook to access your account

Google will ask if you trust Microsoft Outlook. Scroll through the list and click Continue or Allow at the bottom.

10

Account added

You'll be returned to Outlook with the new account in your sidebar. Close Settings.

✓ Done. Your inbox will start populating — new mail arrives instantly, but older messages backfill in the background. Expect the full history to be ready within a few hours, and the weekend at the outside.
11

Send a test email

Click New mail, send a quick message to yourself or a coworker, and confirm it arrives. If it does, you're all set.
